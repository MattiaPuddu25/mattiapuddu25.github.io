Lecture Notes



Here are several notes I wrote while attending various courses. Next to each file, you'll find the last modified date, a download link, and a brief description of the content.

Currently, these notes are in Italian, and some parts are handwritten. I might revise and rewrite them in English in the future; at the same time, I would like to expand them further and possibly consolidate some of them.

A Note on the Dihedral Group 25/07/2019 PDF This note provides a brief presentation of dihedral groups, describing their main properties. The interest in these groups stems from the following fact: for each n ∈ ℕ, with n ≥ 3, the n-th dihedral group is the group of plane transformations that map a given regular n-sided polygon onto itself.

Lie Algebras and Lie Groups 16/06/2021 PDF My notes from the course taught by professors Giovanni Gaiffi and Enrico Le Donne in the academic year 2018/2019.
Main topics of the course: - Lie Algebras: definitions and first properties. Lie Algebra homomorphisms. Simple, semisimple, nilpotent and solvable Lie Algebras. Killing form. - Representation theory of Lie algebras (first part): definitions and first properties. Casimir element. Weyl Theorem. The algebra sl2(K) and its irreducible representations. Standard cyclic modules. Weight lattice and examples. - Root systems: Dynkin diagrams and their complete classification. Weyl groups. Connection between Lie Algebras and root system: Lie theorem and semisimplicity criterion. Toral algebras. - Poincaré-Birkhoff-Witt theorem and Serre relations. - Lie Groups: definitions and first properties. Lie Group homomorphisms. The Lie Algebra of GLn(ℝ). One-parameter subgroups and subalgebras. Exponential map: from Lie groups to associated Lie algebras. Adjoint representation. Baker-Campbell-Hausdorff formula.
Elementary Number Theory 25/06/2024 PDF My notes from the course taught by professor Giuseppe Puglisi in the academic year 2017/2018.
Main topics of the course: - The problem of the distribution of primes: the Prime Number Theorem. Lagrange's four-squares theorem. Quadratic residues. The quadratic reciprocity law. - Quadratic congruences and congruences of higher degree. The structure of multiplicative groups modulo q. - Arithmetic functions. Dirichlet series and infinite products. Asymptotic behavior of arithmetic functions. Mertens and Chebyshev theorems. Dirichlet theorem on prime numbers in arithmetic progressions.
Modular Forms 21/01/2022 PDF My notes from the course taught by professors Andrea Maffei and Davide Lombardo in the academic year 2020/2021.
Main topics of the course: - The modular group SL2(ℤ). Classical modular forms, Fourier expansions. Weight of a modular form. Examples: Eisenstein series, the discriminant. Modular forms of arbitrary weight form a polynomial algebra in the variables E_4, E_6. - Classical examples: theta series associated with plane lattices. Representations of integers as sums of squares. - Generalization to congruence subgroups of SL2(ℤ). Geometric interpretation. - Hecke operators, recurrence relations. Properties of Ramanujan's tau function. The Petersson inner product. Simultaneous eigenvectors for the Hecke operators. - L-functions attached to modular forms. Functional equation in the cuspidal case. Euler product decomposition of the L-function attached to an eigenform.
Elements of Algebraic Topology 12/07/2022 PDF My notes from the course taught by professor Filippo Callegaro in the academic year 2021/2022.
Main topics of the course: - Singular homology: construction and basic properties. Applications. CW-complexes, cellular homology. - Cohomology ring, cup product, Poincaré duality. - Homotopy groups: construction and basic properties. Cellular approximation. Homotopy groups of spheres.
Elements of Complex Analysis 15/02/2022 PDF My notes from the course taught by professor Marco Abate in the academic year 2020/2021.
Main topics of the course: - Complements of complex analysis in one variable: compact-open topology and topology of uniform convergence on compact sets. Convergence of sequences of holomorphic functions; compactness in the space of holomorphic functions. - Hurwitz theorems. Schwarz lemma. Automorphisms of the unit disk, of the half-plane, of the complex plane, of the Riemann sphere. - Poincaré distance. Wolff-Denjoy theorem. Riemann uniformization theorem. Approximation of holomorphic functions; applications. Construction of global functions starting from local data. - Introduction to complex analysis in several variables: definitions and examples. Cauchy-Riemann conditions and consequences. Analytic continuation principle. Cauchy integral formula. Cauchy inequalities. Maximum principle. Weierstrass, Montel and Vitali theorems. Hartogs extension theorem. Domains of holomorphy. Convex and pseudoconvex domains. Plurisubharmonic functions. Levi problem and its solution.

Elements of Algebraic Geometry 18/09/2023 PDF My notes from the course taught by professor Rita Pardini in the academic year 2020/2021.
Main topics of the course: - Projective geometry. Subspaces, projective transformations. Affine charts. Cross-ratio. Plane curves, Bézout's theorem. Plane cubics. Nullstellensatz. - Affine and quasi-projective varieties: Zariski topology, irreducible decomposition, morphisms, rational maps, dimension, tangent spaces. Segre varieties, Veronese varieties, Grassmannians. Dimension of an irreducible quasi-projective variety. - Tangent space and singular points.

Algebraic Geometry C 28/07/2023 PDF A list of definitions and statements from the course taught by professor Marco Franciosi in the academic year 2022/2023.
Main topics of the course: - Definition and examples of Riemann surfaces. Holomorphic and meromorphic functions on Riemann surfaces. Group actions and finite Riemann surfaces; Hurwitz bound for the number of automorphisms of a curve of genus greater than 1. - Differential forms and integration on a Riemann surface. - Divisors on compact Riemann surfaces, principal divisors, linear equivalence, canonical divisors. Linear systems and maps to projective spaces. The Riemann-Roch theorem. Serre duality with applications: embedding compact Riemann surfaces in projective space, curves of low genus. - Projective curves: Castelnuovo's bound, general position lemma, flexes, Weierstrass points. Definition of the Jacobian variety, Abel-Jacobi map. The theorem of Abel and Jacobi.

Groups and Representations 07/11/2022 PDF A list of definitions and statements from the course taught by professors Michele D'Adderio and Giovanni Gaiffi in the academic year 2022/2023.
Main topics of the course: - Representation theory of finite groups: definition, subrepresentation, irreducible representation, complete reducibility, Schur's lemma. - Characters and orthonormality of the characters of irreducible representations. Induction. Representation of associative algebras, semisimple algebras. - Representation of the symmetric group: construction of representations and characters. Schur's duality. Algebraic Representations of the general linear group.

The file includes notes on some theoretical parts concerning induced representations, the Jacobi-Trudi identity, representations of an index 2 subgroup and of GLn(ℂ), as well as a summary table of bases of symmetric function spaces.
Coxeter Groups 03/06/2025 PDF Written in collaboration with Sabrina Botticchio, Alessio Sgubin, Giuseppe Mascellani, Giovanni Marzenta and Mirko Torresani, these are notes from the course taught by professors Michele D'Adderio and Mario Salvetti in the academic year 2023/2024.
Main topics of the course: - Basic theory of Coxeter groups: their presentation, geometric realization, combinatorial characterization. - Associated combinatorial structures: Bruhat order, order complex, root system. - Classification of finite Coxeter groups. - Applications in combinatorics and topology.
Algebraic Number Theory 1 21/02/2023 PDF My notes from the course taught by professors Ilaria Del Corso and Roberto Dvornicich in the academic year 2017/2018.
Main topics of the course: - Integral extensions of a ring. Trace and norm. Additive structure of the ring of integers of a number field. Integral bases. - Dedekind domains. Fractional ideals and group of ideal classes. Unique factorization of ideals in Dedekind domains. - Ramification and inertia of primes in field extensions and Galois extensions. Kummer Theorem. Galois extensions: decomposition and inertia groups. Frobenius morphism. Ramified primes. - Minkowski Convex Body Theorem. The group of ideal classes is finite. Minkowski constant. Dirichlet's unit theorem.
Algebra 13/03/2022 PDF My notes from the course taught by professor Andrea Maffei in the academic year 2019/2020.
Main topics of the course: - Notions of dimension for a commutative ring. Krull's Hauptidealsatz and applications. - Regular sequences, regular local rings, equivalent characterizations. Completion, Artin-Rees lemma. - Basic concepts of homological algebra: complexes, long exact sequences, derived categories, Ext and Tor. Homological dimension.
